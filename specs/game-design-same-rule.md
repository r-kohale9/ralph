# Game Design: "Same Rule?"

## Overview

**Game ID:** same-rule
**Learning Goal:** Ratio Intuition for Grade 5 (Skills 1 + 2)
**Core mechanic:** Compare two real-world scenes and decide if they follow the same "for every" relationship
**Platform:** Mobile-first (vertical layout, responsive)
**Rounds:** 10 (3 stages)
**Lives:** None — low-stakes, exploratory
**Scoring:** Star rating at the end based on accuracy

---

## Learning Objective

The kid anchors the habit of thinking "for every X of this, there are Y of that" when looking at two related quantities. They learn that this "for every" description is more useful than "how many more" — because only the "for every" rule tells you whether two situations match.

---

## Round Flow (Mobile)

### Step 1 — Scene A appears

A real-world visual fills the top portion of the screen. Below it, a natural-language label describes what's shown.

Example: An illustration of a small plate with 2 cookies and 1 glass of milk. Label: "Ria's snack plate: 2 cookies and 1 glass of milk."

**Sound:** A soft, cheerful scene-reveal chime as the illustration animates in.

### Step 2 — Scene B slides in

Scene A compresses slightly upward. Scene B slides in below it. Now both scenes are visible, stacked vertically.

Label: "Ria's party plate: 4 cookies and 2 glasses of milk."

At the bottom, two buttons appear: **"Same Rule"** and **"Different Rule."**

**Sound:** A gentle slide-in whoosh as Scene B appears.

### Step 3 — Kid taps Same Rule or Different Rule

Binary choice. No math yet — just gut feeling from looking at the scenes.

**Sound:** A soft tap/click confirmation when the kid selects a button.

### Step 4 — State the rule (sentence builder)

Both scenes stay visible (compressed) at the top. A sentence builder slides up from the bottom:

**"For every ___ [thing A], there are ___ [thing B]."**

Two small number pickers (1–9) for the blanks. The thing names are pre-filled from the context (e.g., "cookies" and "glasses of milk").

Kid fills in the numbers and taps **"Check."**

**Sound:** Gentle number-tick sounds as the kid scrolls through the picker.

### Step 5 — Feedback

**If correct:**
- Items in both scenes animate into "for every" groups — they visually cluster so the kid sees the pattern (e.g., groups of 2 cookies + 1 milk glass light up with a soft glow)
- A green checkmark appears with a short encouraging line: "That's it! For every 2 cookies, 1 glass of milk — same rule, just bigger!"
- **Sound:** A warm, satisfying "correct" chime — melodic, not jarring.

**If wrong:**
- The correct grouping animates gently (no harsh red X)
- A brief explanation appears in friendly language
- Kid moves on, no penalty
- **Sound:** A soft, neutral "not quite" tone — gentle, encouraging, not punishing.

**Additive trap rounds get an extra explanation:**
After the feedback animation, a callout box appears:
"Notice: both had 2 more birds than squirrels. But the 'for every' rule was different! The difference can trick you — the 'for every' pattern is what really matters."

**Sound:** A subtle "insight" notification sound — like a soft bell — when the explanation appears.

### Transition to next round

A brief pause, then the screen clears and the next round's Scene A slides in.

**Sound:** A light transition swoosh between rounds.

## Round Map

### Stage 1 — Easy Matches (Rounds 1–3)

Both scenes follow the same rule. Kid builds confidence with the "for every" language.

**Round 1 — Cookies and Milk**
- Scene A: A small snack plate with 2 cookies and 1 glass of milk
- Scene B: A party plate with 4 cookies and 2 glasses of milk
- Answer: **Same Rule**
- Rule: For every 2 cookies, there is 1 glass of milk
- Why this context: Every kid has had cookies with milk. The pairing feels natural.

**Round 2 — Flowers in a Garden**
- Scene A: A small flower bed with 3 red flowers and 1 yellow flower
- Scene B: A bigger flower bed with 6 red flowers and 2 yellow flowers
- Answer: **Same Rule**
- Rule: For every 3 red flowers, there is 1 yellow flower
- Why this context: Gardens are familiar, colors make grouping visually obvious.

**Round 3 — Fish in a Tank**
- Scene A: A small fish tank with 4 orange fish and 2 blue fish
- Scene B: A bigger tank with 4 orange fish and 5 blue fish
- Answer: **Different Rule**
- Rule: Scene A is "for every 2 orange, 1 blue" — Scene B doesn't follow this
- Why this context: Kids love fish tanks. The color contrast makes counting easy.

### Stage 2 — The Additive Trap (Rounds 4–7)

Designed to break the "how many more" habit. Some pairs have the same difference but different ratios. Some have different differences but the same ratio.

**Round 4 — Birds and Squirrels in a Park**
- Scene A: A park bench with 3 birds and 1 squirrel (difference = 2)
- Scene B: A bigger park with 5 birds and 3 squirrels (difference = 2)
- Answer: **Different Rule**
- Trap: Same difference (2 more birds), but ratio changed from 3:1 to 5:3
- Explanation: "Both times there were 2 more birds than squirrels. But the 'for every' rule changed! In the small park it was 'for every 3 birds, 1 squirrel.' In the big park it's different."

**Round 5 — Rice and Dal Jars on a Shelf**
- Scene A: A kitchen shelf with 2 jars of rice and 3 jars of dal (difference = 1)
- Scene B: A bigger shelf with 4 jars of rice and 6 jars of dal (difference = 2)
- Answer: **Same Rule**
- Trap: Difference changed (1 → 2), but ratio held (2:3)
- Explanation: "The difference changed — but the 'for every' rule stayed the same! For every 2 jars of rice, there are 3 jars of dal. That's what matters."

**Round 6 — Beads on a Necklace**
- Scene A: A friendship bracelet with 2 gold beads and 1 black bead (difference = 1)
- Scene B: A necklace with 4 gold beads and 3 black beads (difference = 1)
- Answer: **Different Rule**
- Trap: Same difference (1 more gold), but ratio changed from 2:1 to 4:3
- Explanation: "Both had 1 more gold bead than black. But look at the pattern — the bracelet was 'for every 2 gold, 1 black.' The necklace doesn't follow that rule."

**Round 7 — Tables and Chairs in a Classroom**
- Scene A: A small classroom with 1 table and 4 chairs (difference = 3)
- Scene B: A bigger classroom with 3 tables and 12 chairs (difference = 9)
- Answer: **Same Rule**
- Trap: Difference jumped from 3 to 9, but ratio held (1:4)
- Explanation: "Way more extra chairs in the big room! But the rule is the same — for every 1 table, there are 4 chairs. The difference changed, the rule didn't."

### Stage 3 — Kid Leads (Rounds 8–10)

The kid sees ONE scene first, states the rule, THEN the second scene appears and they judge it. This flips from recognition to generation.

**Round 8 — Sunflowers and Pots**
- Scene A: A garden display with 6 sunflowers in 3 pots
- Kid states: "For every 2 sunflowers, there is 1 pot"
- Scene B appears: 9 sunflowers in 3 pots
- Answer: **Different Rule** (now it's 3:1, not 2:1)

**Round 9 — Sugar and Water for Lemonade**
- Scene A: A lemonade recipe showing 2 spoons of sugar and 5 cups of water
- Kid states: "For every 2 spoons of sugar, there are 5 cups of water"
- Scene B appears: 4 spoons of sugar and 10 cups of water
- Answer: **Same Rule** (2:5 scaled up)

**Round 10 — Threads on a Loom**
- Scene A: A weaving pattern with 3 red threads and 2 white threads
- Kid states: "For every 3 red threads, there are 2 white threads"
- Scene B appears: 9 red threads and 6 white threads
- Answer: **Same Rule** (3:2 scaled up)

## Sound Design

| Moment | Sound | Character |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Scene A appears | Soft reveal chime | Cheerful, inviting — like opening a storybook |
| Scene B slides in | Gentle whoosh | Smooth, not startling |
| Button tap (Same/Different) | Soft click | Tactile confirmation |
| Number picker scroll | Light tick | Subtle, like a combination lock |
| Correct answer | Warm melodic chime | Satisfying, rewarding — not over the top |
| Wrong answer | Soft neutral tone | Gentle, "try again" energy — no punishment |
| Grouping animation | Quiet clustering sound | Items gently clicking into place |
| Additive trap explanation | Soft bell/insight ding | "Aha moment" — draws attention to the callout |
| Round transition | Light swoosh | Clean, forward momentum |
| Game end — 3 stars | Celebratory jingle | Short, joyful, earned |
| Game end — 2 stars | Upbeat tune | Encouraging — "great job, play again!" |
| Game end — 1 star | Gentle positive tone | Warm — "you're learning, keep going!" |

---

## Star Rating

- **3 stars:** 8–10 correct (including at least 3 of 4 additive trap rounds)
- **2 stars:** 5–7 correct
- **1 star:** 1–4 correct

Stars are shown at the end screen with a summary: "You spotted the 'for every' rule X out of 10 times!"

---

## Visual Style

- Mobile-first vertical layout
- Real-world illustrated scenes (not abstract shapes)
- Warm, friendly color palette
- Clear visual grouping — items arranged so the "for every" pattern is visible but not highlighted until feedback
- Sentence builder uses large, finger-friendly number pickers
- Buttons are large tap targets (minimum 44px)

---
